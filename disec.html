The Disarmament and International Security Committee

DISEC deals with topics that center around disarmament, global issues, and threats to peace that jeopardize international security. DISEC works closely with the UN Disarmament Commission and the Conference on Disarmament. The Disarmament Commission was created in 1978 as a subsidiary body of the GA. Its mandate is to make recommendations on specific issues of disarmament, and to report annually to the GA. It has historically formed principles and guidelines on a select few topics per year, while drawing upon expert knowledge and relying on international consensus.

Topic 

Situation in Central America

Today the world is witness to a global crisis in the Middle east. While most of the international media attention is on the refugees arriving in Europe – from countries such as Syria, Iraq, and Afghanistan – there is another protection crisis unfolding in Central America. No one knows this better than those fleeing
epidemic levels of violence, including gender-based violence, in El Salvador, Guatemala, and Honduras.
This region of Central America, known as the Northern Triangle (“Northern Triangle of Central America” or “NTCA”), is one of the most dangerous places on earth. According to data from the UN Office on Drugs and Crime, Honduras ranks first, El Salvador fifth, and Guatemala sixth for rates of homicide globally. Furthermore, El Salvador, Guatemala, and Honduras rank first, third, and seventh, respectively, for rates of female homicides globally. Drug trafficking, rival gangs, and rampant corruption all contribute to the high levels of violence. The region has come under increasing control by sophisticated, organized criminal armed groups, often with transnational reach, driving up rates of murder, gender based violence, and other forms of serious harm.
